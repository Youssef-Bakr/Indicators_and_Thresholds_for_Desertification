Project Abstract
Objectives
The mechanisms of land degradation are well known and have been the object of many EU supported studies.

The prime objective of INDEX was to apply this knowledge to develop modern, rapid, sensitive, universal, multivariate indicators via which the dynamic state of land degradation as well as its remediation can be assessed.

They were based on: microbiology including molecular biology and genetic diversity, the characteristics of the dynamic humus pool and humoenzymes, and also on soil physics including rheology.

These indicators of desertification mechanisms were developed on fields in various stages of degradation and remediation and were verified on a pan European basis on other sites selected with stakeholders.

The objectives of INDEX were innovative, since they were based on mechanisms, they were holistic, and they potentially introduced novel parameters for soil quality and remediation assessment. The cost evaluation of suitable indicators was also an objective.

Expected Results
As expected the results showed that universal soil quality indicators were not possible. Even locations in the same region, suffering from similar pressures need to be locally benchmarked.

A factor composed of 6 nongenetic microbial parameters (basal respiration, ATP, microbial biomass, proteins, and the activities of basic phosphatase and ß-glucosidase) as well as the fluorescence efficiency of aqueous extracts was a powerful indicator of degradation and remediation in locations with degradation related to vegetal decline.

The impact of erosion on soil quality could be followed by the concentration of particles smaller than 0.01 mm in a pyrophosphate extract, while an indicator composed of the activities of ß-glucosidase and urease could be used to trace the impact of agricultural practices.

The results are of value to scientists researching soil quality assessment and mechanisms and also to organisations evaluating soil degradation (desertification) and to those supporting remediation efforts, such as the UNCCD and World Bank.
